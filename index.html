The Challenge

Client

Five Guys (General Assembly)

Time

3 weeks

Team

Cherie  Ryan  Jin

My role

Research Ideation  Prototyping & Test  Iteration  Presentation

A General Assembly project where I worked in a team of 3. We all worked together across the stages of the process with the aim to:

Create an intuitive online solution for Five Guys customers that allows them to order remotely, pay, as groups and individuals and more easily split their bill. We had 9 days to produce a high fildelity prototype.

All whilst engaging people with Five Guys core brand attributes and encouraging loyalty.

"We are in the business of  selling burgers"

Five Guys has 27 restaurants across the UK, with one mission. To sell tasty burgers fast. They want to improve their digital ordering to make it more intuitive and enable people to order as groups, considering how they can easily split the bill at the end.

This touchpoint is also an opportunity to increase engagement and loyalty with their customers, so we wanted to ensure we considered this throughout.

The Competition.

We quickly reviewed what the competitors are offering. Ranging from in-store digital touch screens in McDonalds,to specifically designed apps such as the Gourmet Burger Kitchen app.

Whilst there were some good solutions out there nobody has yet provided a fully integrated way to order as a group without staff intervention and a bill splitting solution that requires minimal effort from the consumer.

In terms of positioning against competitors our research showed us that Five Guys can be conceived as

"fast food at restaurant prices"

Contextual research

We wanted to get under the skin of the consumer by understanding their journey and experience in real time. This involved visiting a number of restaurants in the competitive set and crucially Five Guys itself.

We wanted to understand the current ordering system and the specific pain points from the users point of view, both the Five Guys customer and the servers

Alongside the contextual research we began talking to a wider base of users. We sent out a survey with three core objectives:

  • 1) Identify users for in-depth interview and testing  
  • 2) Explore what makes for a good restaurant experience. 
  • 3) Find out about peoples existing experiences of Five Guys 

This feedback gave us clear themes to explore in our user interviews ,which revealed the following themes:

We used the information from the survey and the interviews to build some key personas and help identify the key pain points

Meet our key persona Steve

We built an emotional path for Steve as the most regular and committed Five Guys customer likely to use an online ordering facility.

Steve is stopping at Five Guys with some friends on his way to a gig. Currently this can entail a long queue, confusion around customisation and pressure to order. Orders cannot be made from the table so any extras have to be queued for again.

Features and prioritisation

Using all the information gathered to date we started to plan the functionality and features our solution would need.

We took two of the key features into a design studio to identify the best way to tackle them before moving onto our first prototypes

First we prototyped a mobile app solution. We wanted people to be able to order in advance of entering the restaurant and to have an account so that we could build a relationship with them.

This led to addressing group ordering using one persons phone, the ordering would follow a linear process Eg Burger — fries — drink, this way each order could be made on the same phone but still broken down by person.  Payment could then be made in its entirety by the App account holder or by individuals on their friends phone. Alternatively each individual could download the app and the orders would be seperate. 

When we user tested via a paper prototype, two things became clear.

1/ People did not always want to order in a linear way, eg preferring to add two fries at once etc. 2/ they were not comfortable processing their payment details on someone else’s phone. 

A change of plan

Following this feedback we decided to move the ordering solution into the website ( www.fiveguys.co.uk) so that a cloud based solution could be used.

Each individual could easily use their own phone and link their order together. Taking forward the elements of the app that people liked, such as the easy customisation options, and clear icon orientated menus, we tested this new order process. 

This was well received by users so we moved on to developing the reward and loyalty aspects linked to online ordering accounts.

We focused on a points based loyalty scheme linked to spend to encourage ongoing engagement, we created a build a burger game aimed to kill the average waiting time of 7 minutes between ordering and receiving the burger. And focused on incentivising social sharing.

We also introduced a feature in the ordering process which allows users to add previous orders to their cart from the main menu page, saving the time of going through the whole ordering process again on future visits.
